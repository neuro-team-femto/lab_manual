# Taking guests


Here's a sample email with English and French instructions of how to navigate the train/hotel system + some standard agenda for a 2-day visit. 

## English

```
Here are a few details about your visit to Besançon this week. 

You will be arriving at "Besançon Viotte" station, which is our city-centre station. Be aware that there is another station, called "Besançon Franche-Comté TGV", which is about 10 minutes from the centre. Depending on your trains, your train will either terminate at Viotte (in which case you should not get off at the preceding TGV station), or terminate at BFC TGV, in which case you will have to change for a TER shuttle train, which will terminate at Viotte (usually track #8). The tickets you received should include that shuttle train connection.  

The easiest way to get from Viotte station to your hotel is to take the tramway, which is immediately at the south (‘centre-ville’) exit of the station. There is only one line, with the station as its terminus, so there's no confusion about direction. There are 3 stops to your hotel (IBIS Centre Ville), get off at the République stop and walk for less than a minute. You can pay for the tram directly by beeping a bank card, or by buying a ticket before getting on at the station. 

I'm unfortunately not available to welcome you personally on the evening of Wednesday [...]. Your hotel is right in the centre of town, known here as ‘la boucle’ (because it's in a loop of the river Doubs).You'll find lots of little restaurants around the Place de la Révolution, rue G. Courbet and rue C. Pouillet, as well as under the arcades of Quai Vauban, all within 5 minutes of your hotel. By default, I imagine you'll want a quiet evening after the trip - but if you'd rather ‘get started’ on Wednesday evening (!), I can also organise a dinner with a few colleagues interested in your topic, just let me know what you prefer.  

On Thursday morning, I propose to pick you up at your hotel at 9am, take a quick tour of the city, and then accompany you to the lab by public transport. We're still putting together a programme for the day, but I'll probably accompany you on visits to our automation, optics and mechanics departments, where we work with several colleagues interested in data-driven modelling of dynamic systems. We'll have lunch nearby, then your seminar is from [...] 

On Thursday evening, I propose to organise a dinner, with a group of around 6 people, to round off the day. Let me know if you have any dietary restrictions or preferences. As the weather is a bit cold, I was thinking of going to a cheese restaurant (fondue, etc.), one of the region's specialities. Would that be suitable? 
```

## French

```

Selon vos trains, votre train sera soit terminus Viotte (auquel cas il ne faut pas descendre à la gare TGV qui la précède), soit terminus BFC TGV, auquel cas il vous faudra changer pour une navette TER qui, elle, sera terminus Viotte. 

De la gare Viotte à votre hotel, le plus simple est de prendre le tramway qui se trouve immediatement à la sortie sud ("centre-ville") de la gare. Il n'y a qu'une ligne, dont la gare est le terminus, donc pas de confusion de direction possible. Il y a 3 arrêts jusqu'à votre hotel (IBIS Centre Ville, sauf erreur), descendre à l'arrêt République et mois d'une minute de marche. Vous pouvez payer le tram directement en bipant une carte bancaire, ou bien en achetant un billet avant de monter à la station. 

Je ne suis malheureusement pas disponible le soir du mercredi pour vous accueillir [...]. Votre hôtel est en plein centre-ville, qu'on appelle ici "la boucle" (car inséré dans une boucle de la rivière, le Doubs). Comme la météo s'annonce un peu triste, je pensais par défaut aller vers un restaurant de fromage (fondue, etc.), une des spécialités de la région. Est-ce que cela pourrait convenir ? 

Voilà pour l'instant. Nous nous réjouissons de vous accueillir cette semaine !
```
